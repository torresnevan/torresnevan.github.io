AI and Plagiarism

AI Detection and Plagiarism Concerns

AI Detection Tools

As the writing and comprehension capabilities of AI has grown, students have become increasingly reliant on AI in their academic work. To counter this issue, new AI detection and plagiarism software has recently emerged. Tools such as TurnItIn's AI Writing Indicator and GPTZero work by evaluating student writing against a wide array of AI characteristics such as uniformity, variance, perplexity, and burstiness. Through the evaluation of these statistics, AI detectors can evaluate whether a student's work was truly their own. While no AI checker is 100% accurate, educators can use these tools to filter which student's work they should personally review.

Understanding AI-Generated Content

Academic Integrity

Students using AI in their coursework must remember AI should support learning, not replace it. Assignments are meant to build critical thinking and problem solving skills, students lose these opportunities for growth when they misuse AI tools. AI usage without proper disclosure can count as plagiarism, a serious academic offense that can significantly limit a student's education. Students who use AI put their honest classmates at a severe disadvantage, creating an unfair learning environment.
